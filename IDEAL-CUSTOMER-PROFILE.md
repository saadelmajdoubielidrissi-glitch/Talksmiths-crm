## ICP Summary

Your ideal customer is a **mid-to-large Moroccan company (50-500 employees, MAD 10M-500M revenue)** with international exposure that must communicate effectively with English-speaking clients, partners, or stakeholders. The primary verticals are Business Process Outsourcing (BPO) centers, IT outsourcing firms, tech startups serving global markets, and multinational subsidiaries operating in Morocco's free zones (Casablanca Finance City, Technoparks).

These companies face a critical pain point: they have **technically skilled talent that lacks the executive-level English proficiency** required to close deals, manage international client relationships, and participate effectively in global business contexts. With Morocco's outsourcing sector generating £1.9 billion annually and serving 1,200+ international companies, the demand for Business English coaching has never been higher. The timing is particularly favorable as companies shift from transactional outsourcing relationships to strategic partnerships requiring higher-level communication skills.

The ideal buyer is a CEO, HR Director, or VP of Operations at a growth-stage company who recognizes that English proficiency gaps are limiting their ability to win larger contracts, expand into English-speaking markets, or elevate their talent to client-facing roles. They need measurable ROI, flexible scheduling for busy executives, and training that delivers visible results within 60-90 days.

## Pain Point Map

### Pain Point 1: Client Communication Breakdowns
- **Severity:** Critical
- **Manifests As:** Misunderstandings in email threads, awkward client calls, lost RFP opportunities due to weak proposal language, clients requesting "a native English speaker" instead of current team member
- **Business Impact:** Lost contracts worth $50K-$500K annually; delayed payments due to communication confusion; client churn to competitors with better English capabilities
- **Trigger Events:** Lost major client deal where language was a factor; negative client feedback mentioning communication; failed international expansion attempt
- **Current Workaround:** Hiring expensive native English consultants for key calls; asking bilingual employees to translate; using Google Translate for important emails
- **Your Solution Angle:** Executive coaching that builds authentic confidence for client interactions, not just grammar correction

### Pain Point 2: Talent Stuck in Back-Office Roles
- **Severity:** High
- **Manifests As:** Technical talent with 5+ years experience still not client-facing; high-potential employees plateauing due to language limitations; internal frustration about promotion criteria
- **Business Impact:** Inability to scale client-facing teams; over-reliance on a few bilingual executives; employee turnover of top technical talent seeking growth elsewhere
- **Trigger Events:** Internal promotion review cycles; key bilingual employee leaving; company restructuring to add client-facing layers
- **Current Workaround:** Promoting only employees with international degrees; sending talent to expensive immersion programs abroad; accepting suboptimal client coverage ratios
- **Your Solution Angle:** Structured executive coaching that prepares talent for specific client-facing scenarios with measurable milestones

### Pain Point 3: Executive Presentation Limitations
- **Severity:** High
- **Manifests As:** CEO/CTO deferring to junior English-speaking staff for international board meetings; avoiding speaking opportunities at international events; weak English-language investor updates
- **Business Impact:** Lower valuation multiples; difficulty raising international capital; missed thought leadership opportunities; inability to represent company at highest levels
- **Trigger Events:** Upcoming fundraising round; scheduled international conference speaking slot; preparation for M&A discussions; board diversity initiatives
- **Current Workaround:** Pre-scripting every presentation word-for-word; bringing translators to meetings; recording presentations and having them proofread
- **Your Solution Angle:** High-touch executive coaching focusing on impromptu speaking, Q&A handling, and authentic executive presence in English

### Pain Point 4: Cultural Intelligence Gaps
- **Severity:** Medium-High
- **Manifests As:** Misunderstanding Western business etiquette (small talk, directness, humor); awkward video call dynamics; difficulty building rapport with English-speaking clients
- **Business Impact:** Relationships remain transactional instead of strategic; lower Net Promoter Scores from English-speaking clients; difficulty expanding beyond French-speaking markets
- **Trigger Events:** Opening new market (UK, US, Gulf States); negative cultural feedback from client satisfaction surveys; employee relocating to international offices
- **Current Workaround:** Hiring cultural consultants separately from language training; trial-and-error in client relationships; assigning French-speaking clients to certain team members
- **Your Solution Angle:** Integrated Business English + Cultural Intelligence coaching (a key differentiator from competitors)

## Negative ICP (Who to Avoid)

### 1. Domestic-Only Companies
- **Red Flag:** 100% of revenue from Moroccan clients; no international presence planned
- **Why:** Business English is unnecessary overhead for these companies

### 2. Early-Stage Startups (<2 years, <10 employees)
- **Red Flag:** Pre-revenue or minimal revenue; founder-focused
- **Why:** Can't afford team training; individual coaching better fit

### 3. State-Owned Enterprises (SOEs)
- **Red Flag:** Complex procurement processes; multiple approval layers; long sales cycles
- **Why:** 12-18 month sales cycles; heavy bureaucracy; price-driven decisions

### 4. Companies in Financial Distress
- **Red Flag:** Layoffs, office closures, delayed salary payments (check LinkedIn/Glassdoor)
- **Why:** Training budget first to be cut; high churn risk

### 5. Individual Consumers
- **Red Flag:** Personal email domains; individual LinkedIn messages
- **Why:** Different pricing model, different sales process

### 6. Companies with Deep Berlitz/EF Lock-in
- **Red Flag:** Multi-year contracts with major providers; dedicated training facilities
- **Why:** High switching costs; limited flexibility

### 7. Teams with Native English Speakers
- **Red Flag:** Leadership team includes native English speakers; international offices with English as working language
- **Why:** No training need already solved

### 8. Price-Shopping Prospects
- **Red Flag:** First question is "what's your hourly rate?"; comparing to group classes
- **Why:** Don't value executive coaching differentiation

### 9. Remote/Rural Locations
- **Red Flag:** No presence in Casablanca/Rabat/Tangier; >3 hours travel time
- **Why:** In-person component difficult; lower concentration of ideal clients

### 10. Unrealistic Expectations
- **Red Flag:** "Fluent in 3 months"; "Need C1 level immediately"; no commitment to practice
- **Why:** Coaching requires time and effort; these clients churn
