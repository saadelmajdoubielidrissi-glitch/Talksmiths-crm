## 2. Discovery & Precision: Adjective Mastery (25 mins)
**Objective:** Correct usage of -ing vs. -ed and Hyphenated Shortcuts.

### 2.1 The Receiver vs. The Cause (-ed/-ing)
Fill in the gaps for this email from a CEO to her team:
> "Team, I know the last quarter has been (1) ___________ (exhaust). Many of you feel (2) ___________ (exhaust), but the results are (3) ___________ (motivate). I am (4) ___________ (amaze) by your resilience. The market trends are (5) ___________ (alarm), but our (6) ___________ (motivate) partners are (7) ___________ (excite) about our new project."

### 2.2 The Hyphenated Shortcut
Combine the items to create compound adjectives for these situations:
- A strategy for the next 5 years: A ___________ strategy.
- A solution that saves a lot of money: A ___________ solution.
- A change made at the last possible moment: A ___________ change.
- A culture that cares about performance: A ___________ culture.

## 3. Introduction to Flow: Strategic Linking (25 mins)
**Objective:** Connecting complex ideas with professional discourse markers.

**The "Contrast & Addition" Drill:**
Join these two ideas using the word in brackets. You may need to change the sentence structure.

1. The initial investment was high. We expect a full return by Q3. (**Although**)
2. We need to improve staff training. We must increase employee retention. (**Moreover**)
3. The dollar is strong. Our imports are cheaper this month. (**Because of**)
4. The client is unhappy with the delay. They have agreed to the new timeline. (**Nevertheless**)

**Speaking Challenge (2 mins):** 
Anita, explain the pros and cons of "The Four-Day Work Week" using at least 3 linking words from the table: *On the one hand, Furthermore, Despite, Consequently.*

## 4. Interaction: Negotiation & Question Tags (25 mins)

2. The price includes onboarding. (*..., doesn't it?*)
3. We won't miss the 30-day cure period. (*..., will we?*)

## 6. Creative Production & Polish: Branding (10 mins)
**Objective:** Applying "Intensifiers" and "Mitigators" for nuance.

**The Nuance Test:**
Your CEO is worried about a 2% drop in sales. Choose the right word to describe it:
- "The drop is **(slightly / utterly)** concerning."
- "The impact is **(somewhat / severely)** damaging to our reputation."
- "The team is **(absolutely / fairly)** committed to fixing it."

**Final Pitch:**
Choose one of these: *A luxury watch* OR *A high-end gym*. 
Pitch it to me in 3 sentences using:
1. One **Intensifier** (e.g., highly effective).
2. One **Compound Adjective** (e.g., Swiss-made).
3. One **Discourse Marker** (e.g., All things considered).
